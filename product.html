Description

Liberty is a late-season dessert apple bred for resistance to scab and the four major apple diseases. The tree forms a rounded canopy and bears heavily and reliably from a young age. Fruit is medium-sized, deep red over a greenish ground; the flesh is crisp and juicy with a refreshing sweet-tart flavour. Excellent fresh, for juice and cider, and it stores well for two to three months when kept cool.

Breeder: Cornell University · Introduced 1978

Growing & care

On semi-dwarfing MM.106 rootstock the tree grows 3–4 m tall and wide — easy to harvest and maintain. Plant in full sun in well-drained soil; water regularly and train for shape in the early years. The variety is self-sterile, so it needs another apple variety nearby that flowers in the same period to set fruit. Thanks to its built-in disease resistance, Liberty is ideal for a low-spray, organic orchard.
